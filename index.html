Automated Cotton Fiber Quality Reports

Code-Driven Precision Agriculture

Amandeep Kaur Dhaliwal | UGA Bastos Lab

When growers ask "How did fiber quality look in my field last year?", the answer is never simple. Using R and Quarto, I've built an automated reporting system that transforms complex spatial data into clear, customized reports for each grower. One workflow generates multi-year, multi-field summaries with field-level maps and actionable insights — making fiber quality data accessible, scalable, and grower-focused.

McCormick Field Cotton Fiber Quality Report

Screven County, Georgia • 2023

McCormick Field - 2023 Season

📍 Screven County, Georgia 📅 2023 Cotton Quality Project 🏆 Top Performer

🌟 Outstanding Performance Highlights

  • Highest Cotton Quality Score among all Georgia and Texas fields evaluated
  • 100% of samples in high strength categories (≥29 g/tex)
  • 63.3% Very Strong fibers - excellent durability for premium markets
  • 73% Long fibers (1.11–1.26 in) - superior length for high-quality spinning
  • 83% High uniformity - strong fiber consistency across entire field
  • Exceeded both Georgia and Texas state benchmarks

This report demonstrates the power of automated, data-driven reporting for precision agriculture. Using spatial sampling, terrain analysis, and multi-trait evaluation, the system provides growers with clear insights into field-level performance and market positioning.
